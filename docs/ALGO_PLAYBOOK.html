How a pick earns its number

A pick's score is the credibility of the people standing behind it: 10 for an unknown name, up past 100 when ranked winners lead the way. This is the complete story of where that number comes from, in plain betting language.

Current as of July 9, 2026 (evening) · reflects the Wilson percentile rework (capper rank drives every point, the flat base is gone, sources no longer score, full influence reserved for the top 1 percent of the ranking) and the CA line lock (the official line is the market line at 90 minutes before start, or when the pick reaches gold, whichever comes first)
01

The big picture

The score answers one question: how much verified evidence stands behind this side of this game today? Not hype, not follower counts, not which chat room it appeared in. Evidence means graded results in our own ledger.

Three rules shape everything underneath:

RuleWhat it means
Credibility is earnedEvery capper, expert, wallet, and source starts near zero and earns points only through picks we graded ourselves. A famous handle walks in as a credible newcomer, never as a star.
Points are never subtractedNothing ever removes points from a pick. When a losing capper backs a side, the penalty lands as bonus points on the opposite side instead (chapter 3, fade points).
One scale everywhereThe picks list, the game popup, the detail page, and the history archive all show the same number for the same pick. 100 or more is gold. 75 to 99 is silver.
There is no base anymore. The old flat 45 that every pick started with is gone. It never carried information, and worse, it let weak picks sit closer to the tiers than they deserved. Now a pick from a nobody is worth 10, and everything above that has to be earned by the ranked people backing it. A number can only be big because somebody credible made it big.
02

Where picks come from

Four systems feed the board. All of them are free to run, and all of them are held to the same standard: we grade every pick ourselves against the final score. Nobody gets to bring their own record.

SourceWhoHow often we checkNotes
DiscordCappers posting in the tracked channelsInstantly, the moment a message postsAn AI reader extracts the pick, the team, and the capper name from each message.
Action NetworkAbout 52 public expertsEvery 10 minutes during the dayTheir site record is shown on profiles for context but never counts toward scoring.
PolymarketTop 50 traders by lifetime profitEvery 15 minutesReal money positions of 200 dollars or more on game markets count as picks. Bet size versus their usual is logged for future study.
CoversTop 50 contest players (King of Covers)Every 30 minutesContest picks lock at game start, so pending picks are provably pregame.

House rules for every source

Pregame only. A pick counts for the board only if the source's own timestamp is before the game started. Anything placed mid game is kept for the capper's record but never touches the board.

One person, one voice. If the same capper shows up on the same side of the same game from two systems, that is one pick with two receipts, not two picks. The systems can never stack points by echoing each other.

03

The score recipe

Seven ingredients, all additive. The width of each bar shows how much of the total that ingredient can contribute at most.

Best backer0 to 95

The engine. Every capper is ranked on one board by the Wilson score (chapter 4), and their percentile position decides what their picks are worth: the #1 capper delivers 95 points just by posting, a top 1 percent capper 76 to 95, a mid-pack capper around 15, a bottom-quarter capper nothing. The strongest ranked person on the pick sets this number.

A pick whose capper we cannot identify, or whose capper has no graded record yet, is worth a flat 10. Sources are not people: the Action Network pipe, the Discord rooms, the Polymarket firehose earn nothing as entities anymore. Only the individuals inside them do.

Backer stackopen, tapered hard

What the other backers chip in: half of their own worth, tapering by pairs, and only if they have 20 or more graded bets with us. Full detail in chapter 5.

Sport rank bonus0, 10, or 20

Specialists get extra. Each sport keeps its own Wilson ranking, and when the pick's best backer is that sport's #1 or top 5 percent, the pick gets +20. Top 25 percent gets +10. This one has no volume cap by design: a 5-0 tennis specialist sitting on top of the tennis board collects the full bonus.

Market signals0 to 8

Three small, independent checks against real markets: prediction markets pricing this side higher than the sportsbook implies (up to 5), the betting line having moved toward this side since morning (2), and 65 percent or more of public tickets sitting on the other side (1). Deliberately small caps for now. The full values are logged so their weights can be re-fit on evidence later.

Side lean0 to 5

The replacement for the old automatic home team bonus. Each night the system measures whether home or away sides have actually been more profitable per sport over the last 120 days (minimum 100 graded picks each way) and leans that direction. Tennis and golf are excluded because their home and away labels are just listing order.

Sport bonus0 or 5

A flat 5 for the major markets: MLB, NBA, NFL, NCAAF, CBB, NHL, tennis, golf, soccer. WNBA currently gets no sport bonus.

Fade points0 to 8, plus offset

When a capper on the official fade list (chapter 4) backs one side, the opposite side receives 3 to 8 bonus points. Scaled by how much we have seen that capper in this sport, so a losing MLB record fades MLB picks but says almost nothing about their rare NBA pick. If a proven capper is standing on the faded side, that side gets an offsetting bonus too. Nothing is ever taken away from anyone.

The totals gate. One extra check for over/under picks only: a pick cannot be tracked as gold if its best backer has a losing record specifically on totals. Board totals have historically bled money, so gold totals require a backer who has actually won them.
04

How a capper earns points

Every graded pick lands in a permanent ledger that survives the daily reset. Each night at 5:20am the system re-ranks every capper from that full ledger. The ranking is one number with a long pedigree: the Wilson score, the same math Reddit uses to rank comments so that 900 upvotes out of 1,000 beats 3 out of 3.

1. The worst-case win rate

The Wilson score asks: given this record, what is the LOWEST the capper's true win rate could plausibly be? We use the cautious 99 percent version. A 7-0 heater has a great face but a thin file, so its worst case is modest. An 85-49 grind over 134 bets has a worst case that is genuinely strong. That is why MidwestMike ranks #1 overall while perfect little records sit below him. Volume is not a bonus; it is the proof that makes the number trustworthy.

2. One board, every capper

Everyone with at least one graded decision goes into a single ranking: Discord cappers, Action Network experts, Polymarket wallets, Covers contest players, all treated identically. Your percentile position on that board maps to points through the ladder:

Where you rankYour picks are worth
Top 1%95 down to 76
1 to 5%75 down to 66
5 to 15%65 down to 51
15 to 25%50 down to 41
25 to 35%40 down to 31
35 to 45%30 down to 21
45 to 75%20 down to 11
Bottom 25%0
No graded record / unknown name10 flat

Points slide inside each band, so the top of the 5-15 band is worth 65 and the bottom of it 51. Higher rank always means more points, with no cliffs.

3. Thin records are capped, not banned

A hot 7-0 can rank high on the board, but what its picks can collect is capped until the sample earns trust: under 10 graded decisions caps at 50 points, 10 to 29 caps at 70, and 30 or more collects the full ladder value. Nobody golds a pick off a lucky week.

4. The break-even gate

Ranking high is evidence you exist; handing out points also requires evidence you win. The Wilson score rewards volume, so a capper with a long losing record can still out-rank a crowd of thin ones. The gate stops that from mattering: a capper's win rate (shrunk toward the coin flip on small samples) must clear 50 percent for their backing to be worth more than the unknown-name 10, and full value returns at 53 percent, roughly break-even at standard juice. Between the two it scales smoothly. A 31-33 capper can sit wherever volume ranks them; their picks still collect 10, chip in nothing, and earn no sport bonus until the record actually wins.

5. The fade list

Being ranked low is not enough to get faded; a thin record and a bad record are different things. Fading requires both a bottom-quarter rank and a genuinely losing win rate:

FlagRequirementEffect
FADE WATCHbottom 25%, win% 45 or under, 5+ betsTheir picks contribute nothing.
FADE ACTIVEbottom 25%, win% 40 or under, 15+ betsTheir picks add points to the opposite side.

Sources are no longer cappers

The old system tracked each pipe (Action Network as a whole, each Discord room) as a pseudo-capper that could carry picks by itself. That ended on July 9: the Action Network pipe had quietly pooled enough volume to out-rank every human being we track, and it was minting golds off single unproven backers. Pipes deliver picks; only people earn points.

05

Consensus: the chip-in

The question consensus answers: when several tracked cappers land on the same side, how much extra is that agreement worth? The answer went through two designs in one day, and the second exists because the first failed in public.

The rule

The strongest backer counts in full. Every other backer chips in half of their own worth, with two brakes:

The pair taper. Within each ranking band, the first two extra backers chip in half their worth, the next two a quarter, the two after that an eighth. Agreement is worth something; an echo chamber is not worth an echo chamber's size.

The 20-bet floor. A backer needs 20 or more graded bets with us before their chip-in counts at all. Their own picks still score and they still rank; they just cannot boost anyone else's pick until they have a real file. This one rule is what separates "three proven winners agree" from "a dozen anonymous wallets piled on".

CaseChip-in
A 70-point capper with 40 graded bets joins+35
A second 70-point capper from the same band joins+35, then the band tapers
A hot 6-1 wallet joins (under 20 bets)0 until they hit 20
An unknown or bottom-quarter capper joins0
A fade-list capper joins0, always (and may fade the pick's opposite side)
Why the floor exists. The first Wilson rescore on July 9 let any ranked backer chip in. Within the hour a tennis pick had collected 22 backers, most of them thin Polymarket wallets riding 5-1 style records, and hit 349 points; the board carried 13 golds, more than the crowding this rework was built to kill. With the pair taper and the 20-bet floor the same board settled to roughly 6 golds, every one led by a top-band capper. Verified on the live data before it shipped.
06

The reveal

The true score updates internally the instant anything changes. What members see is deliberately different: any jump bigger than 25 points reveals in 2 to 5 random-sized chunks at random times. A pick worth 85 might appear at 60, sit for a few minutes, jump 9, then 12, then land its last 4. The chunk schedule is generated per pick, so no two picks climb the same way, and the big number and the conviction curve on the detail page replay the exact same steps. One story, two views.

The clock matters

  • 3+ hours outLeisurely reveal, 5 to 30 minutes. There is time to be dramatic.
  • 1 to 3 hoursFast reveal, 90 seconds to 5 minutes. The window to act is shrinking.
  • Under 1 hourInstant. Members always get the full number with a real window to place the bet.

Every reveal finishes at least 3 minutes before first pitch, tip, or kick. And the reveal only ever climbs. Nothing shown ever goes down.

Why it exists: a score that jumps 40 points the instant a top capper posts is a free relay service for anyone screenshotting the board. The climb keeps the edge inside the product while still getting members the full picture in time to use it.

07

Gold, silver, and the record

TierLineTreatment
Gold100+The tracked tier. Saved permanently, graded publicly, and it is the record the site stands on. Totals must also pass the totals gate.
Silver75 to 99Styled on the board so strong picks stand out. Never tracked, never part of the public record.
Archive55+Quietly saved into the permanent pick history for research and capper grading. Not a public tier.

Gold is intentionally hard, and under the Wilson engine it can only happen two ways: a top-band capper posts (the #1 capper alone is 95, plus the sport bonuses), or ranked, volume-proven cappers genuinely stack on one side. A crowd of strangers cannot do it anymore. On the first reranked board, that meant roughly a handful of golds on a full slate, every one led by a top-band name.

A standing alarm watches the gold tier's trailing 30-day record. If gold ever stops beating the old system's benchmark, that alarm is the signal to recalibrate, and it is checked on real results, not vibes.

08

A day in the life

  • 12:30am ETThe pick cycle opens. Overnight posts count toward the coming day.
  • 4:58amFinal grading pass for late West Coast games, then the daily reset wipes the board. Permanent ledgers (capper history, pick history, gold record) are never touched.
  • 5:00amMorning build: game slate from ESPN, betting lines, the six pick slots per game, then a full re-scan of overnight messages.
  • 5:05amSource refresh: Action Network roster, Polymarket wallet list, Covers contestants.
  • 5:20amThe nightly re-rank. Every capper's Wilson score, board position, ladder points, sport ranks, and fade flags recompute from the full graded ledger. This is the moment yesterday's results become today's scoring power.
  • All dayDiscord picks land instantly. Action Network polls every 10 minutes, Polymarket every 15, Covers every 30. Every arrival rescores its pick slot and, if needed, starts a reveal.
  • 4:00pmAfternoon line refresh for evening games.
  • The line lockEach game's CA line locks at whichever comes first: 90 minutes before it starts, or the moment a pick on it reaches CA pick status. Whatever the market shows then becomes the official line: the number the rankings display and every tracked bet grades against. Before that a pick shows the morning line as a placeholder; once locked it never moves. The 90-minute mark follows the real start, so a schedule slip moves with it.
  • Every 5 minLive scores update and finished games get graded, feeding straight back into capper ledgers.

Glossary

ML (moneyline)
A bet on who wins the game, no point spread involved. Soccer moneylines have three outcomes; a draw grades both sides as losses.
Spread
A bet on the margin of victory. Brewers -1.5 means they must win by 2 or more.
O/U (over/under, totals)
A bet on combined points scored, over or under a line like 8.5. The line is the number; the juice is the price you pay to bet it (often -110 or -115).
Odds, American
-131 means risk 131 to win 100. +150 means risk 100 to win 150. Negative numbers are favorites.
Units
Profit measured in equal-sized bets at real odds. The site's honest currency for records: it accounts for whether wins came on favorites or underdogs.
ROI (return on investment)
Units won divided by units risked, as a percentage. A +8% ROI capper makes 8 cents per dollar bet over time.
Wilson score
The lower bound of the 99 percent confidence interval on a win rate: the worst the record could plausibly be. The site's one ranking number. Volume raises it; thin perfection does not.
Band
Where a capper's rank falls on the points ladder (top 1%, 1-5%, 5-15%, and so on). The band plus the position inside it decides what their picks are worth.
Volume cap
The lid on what a thin record can collect: under 10 graded decisions caps at 50 points, 10 to 29 at 70, 30 or more uncapped.
Best backer
The strongest ranked capper on a pick. Their ladder points, in full, are the pick's foundation.
Chip-in
What each additional backer adds: half of their own worth, tapered in pairs per band, and only once they have 20 or more graded bets.
Shrunk record (blend)
A capper's ROI after mixing in a skeptical baseline of imaginary break-even picks. Still used for the totals gate and fade routing details.
Source entity
A pipe (Action Network as a whole, a Discord room) formerly tracked as a pseudo-capper. Retired from scoring on July 9; shown in ops tooling only.
Fade
Betting against someone. A fade-active capper's picks add points to the opposite side of whatever they back.
Slot
One of six bettable sides per game the board pre-creates: home ML, away ML, home spread, away spread, over, under. Picks attach to slots.
Mention
One capper backing one slot, from any source. A slot's score recomputes on every new mention.
Leak rule / reveal
The chunked climb from chapter 6. The public number rises in random steps and finishes before game start; the true score stays internal until revealed.
Conviction curve
The score-over-time chart on a pick's detail page. Replays the exact reveal steps the big number walked.
Steam
The betting line moving toward a side since morning, usually meaning real money arrived on it.
Contrarian
Backing the side most public tickets are against. Historically the public loses slightly more than it wins.
Notional
The dollar size of a Polymarket trade. Each wallet's bet size versus their own usual is logged today, worth zero points until tested.
Grading
Marking a pick win, loss, or push from the final score. We grade everything ourselves; no source's claimed record is ever trusted.
