What is CappingAlpha?

Sports betting is an information problem. The books have it. Most bettors do not. CappingAlpha closes that gap by deploying a network of specialized AI agents trained on years of player data, historical outcomes, and public market behavior. The result is a single ranked list of the day's highest-conviction plays, scored against the morning opening line before a game kicks off.

We are not a pick service. We do not tell you what to bet. We process publicly available information, normalize it through a proprietary scoring engine, and present it in a structured format so you can see exactly where public conviction is concentrated and how it stacks up against the market. You make the call. We just make sure you're informed when you do.

CappingAlpha never wagers on any game. All performance data shown is hypothetical and for informational purposes only. Past results are not indicative of future outcomes.

How the Scoring Model Works

Each morning at 5am ET, book lines (moneylines, spreads, over/unders) are locked from The Odds API as the reference baseline for the day. From there, AI agents monitor public data streams in real time, cross-referencing signals against live game data from ESPN, weather from OpenWeatherMap, public sentiment from X, community intelligence sources, and more. Every signal is normalized, weighted, and run through the scoring engine before a pick appears on the board.

Picks are ranked by score. A higher score means more independent sources aligned on the same side at the same time. The model is calibrated to detect that pressure early, before it moves a line. No single source can spike a score on its own.

Transparency and Disclaimers

Every MVP pick is logged with the final game score and result. The hypothetical P/L shown in the MVP tab assumes a flat $20 unit against the locked morning line. No actual money is wagered. CappingAlpha does not place bets on any game, ever.

Scores generated by CappingAlpha are produced by an AI model trained on public capper data. A high score means more community agreement on a given side. It does not mean that side is likely to cover or win. Past hypothetical performance is not indicative of future results.
